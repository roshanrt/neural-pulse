VPN marketing is some of the most dishonest in tech. Sponsored reviews, recycled benchmarks, and affiliate conflicts cloud every recommendation. This test was done independently, with no affiliate relationships to any of the 11 services tested.

## Testing methodology

Each VPN was tested over 7 days across three locations (Mumbai, London, Singapore) on a 1Gbps fiber connection. Tests included:

- **Speed:** Download/upload via fast.com and iperf3, 10 samples per location
- **DNS leak:** dnsleaktest.com extended test, both connected and during reconnection
- **Kill switch:** Network killed mid-connection, tested whether traffic leaked before kill switch engaged
- **WebRTC leak:** Browser-based leak test in Chrome and Firefox
- **No-log claims:** Jurisdiction, past audits, warrant canary status

## Top picks

### Best overall: Mullvad

Mullvad doesn't ask for an email address during signup. You get an account number. That's it. No personal data collected, no payment linked to identity (they accept cash). Speed is the best in the test. DNS leak protection is airtight.

The interface is minimal but functional. If you want the most private option available, Mullvad is it.

**Price:** €5/month flat. No annual discount because they don't want to lock you in.

### Best balance of speed and trust: ProtonVPN

Proton's infrastructure is Swiss-based with a strong legal privacy framework. The open-source apps have been audited. Speed is excellent, second only to Mullvad. The free tier is genuinely usable (1 device, slower speeds, no P2P).

**Price:** From $4/month (annual).

### Best for streaming: NordVPN

If your primary use case is accessing geo-restricted content, NordVPN's obfuscated servers and wide server network are hard to beat. Netflix, BBC iPlayer, Disney+ — all worked during testing. Speed is solid.

Trust concern: NordVPN had a server breach in 2018. They've since completed multiple audits and improved infrastructure significantly, but worth knowing.

**Price:** From $3.69/month (2-year plan).

## What to avoid

**Hotspot Shield** — DNS leaks detected on 2 separate test days. Deal breaker.

**CyberGhost** — Owned by Kape Technologies, which has a history of distributing adware. No independent audit.

**Free VPNs not on this list** — If the product is free and you're not sure of the business model, you are the product.

## The bottom line

For most users: **ProtonVPN**. It's the best combination of verified privacy, speed, and a trustworthy company with a clear business model.

For maximum privacy: **Mullvad**. No email, no personal data, no questions.

For streaming: **NordVPN**. Best server coverage, acceptable trust level after audits.
