The original Apple Vision Pro launched at $3,499 and weighed 600 grams — enough to cause neck strain during extended use. According to supply chain sources corroborated by two independent analysts, Vision Pro 2 addresses both problems directly.

## What the leaks say

### Weight reduction

The headline spec: Vision Pro 2 is reportedly **40% lighter** than its predecessor, targeting a target weight of approximately 360 grams. This is achieved through:

- A new carbon fiber and titanium composite chassis (replacing the aluminum frame)
- Smaller micro-OLED displays with higher pixel density (reduces the optical stack thickness)
- Redesigned battery system

### The external battery redesign

The original's external battery pack connected via a proprietary cable and lasted about 2 hours. The V2 reportedly moves to a **modular magnetic battery** that clips to clothing rather than dangling from a cable. Capacity is said to increase to 3.5 hours on a single charge, with hot-swappable capability — swap the battery without removing the headset.

### Custom silicon: M4 Ultra variant

The R1 chip from V1 handled real-time sensor processing. V2 reportedly pairs an **M4 Ultra** variant (currently in Mac Pro) with a next-generation R2 chip. The R2 chip is said to reduce display latency from 12ms to under 8ms — perceptually significant for reducing motion sickness.

### Price

The most interesting leak: target price is reportedly **$2,799**, a $700 reduction from the original. Supply chain consolidation and higher volume production are cited as the reasons.

## What this means for the market

The original Vision Pro sold an estimated 500,000 units in its first year — below internal Apple projections. A 40% weight reduction and $700 price drop changes the addressable market significantly.

The more important number is enterprise adoption. Companies that piloted Vision Pro for training, remote collaboration, and CAD visualization cited weight as the primary obstacle to extended use. At 360 grams, that barrier largely disappears.

## Caveats

These leaks come from supply chain sources, not Apple insiders. Apple has demonstrated it can control leaks better than almost any company. Treat specifications as directionally accurate, not confirmed.

No launch date has been reported. Based on historical patterns, a spring 2027 announcement is plausible.

## The bigger picture

Apple is playing a long game in spatial computing. Vision Pro V1 was a technology demonstration at a premium price. V2 with these specs starts to look like a product Apple expects people to actually wear for hours. That's a different business.
